Education

Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering, School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, University Science of Malaysia (USM), 2018.

My Ph.D. thesis is titled: "Adaptive Local Fuzzy Based Region Determination Image Enhancement Techniques for Non-uniform Illumination and Low Contrast Images". It was mainly focusing on image processing and computer vision, based on AI (artificial Intelligent) and Fuzzy Logic.

Motivation

Medical images (microscopic, x-ray and MRI images) might get affected by the issue of non-uniform illumination and low contrast, which might affect important informations inside those images. Therefore, my Ph.D. thesis came to solve this issue with the following objectives:

Objectives

1- To detect the early stages of breast cancer by enhancing the biomedical x-ray images and to detect the defect in human sperm by processing the microscopic images.
2- To determine the non-uniform illumination and low contrast images into three main regions (over-exposed, well-exposed, and under-exposed regions).
3- To enhance each region individually without coming across the other regions.
4- To combine the three enhanced regions to be the uniform output image.

contributions

This study came out with four main contributions:
1- A new algorithm titled: Adaptive Local Exposure Based Region Determination.
2- A new algorithm titled: Adaptive Fuzzy Exposure Local Contrast Enhancement.
3- Enhancment and determination system for the non-uniform illumination and low contrast images.
4- Non-uniform illumination and low contrast image: Systematic Review (research article).

During the Ph.D. research, I have processed over 1000 images . All those images are of non-uniform and low contrast. My Ph.D. was done under the supervision of Professor Ir. Dr. Nor Ashidi Mat Isa

MS.c. in Electrical and Computer Engineering, School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, University Science of Malaysia (USM), 2014.

My MS.c. degree was in the field of digital signal processing (DSP) based on Machine Learning (ML), focusing on voice recognition-based biometrics and bioacoustics. My MS.c. degree research helped the medical field in developing a new antibiotic for skin cancer, using rare species of frogs as an animal model. In this study I build and installed a biometric and bioacoustics voice recognition system in a jungle area, in-order to detect, find , and catch those rare frongs.

Motivation

Nine rare specieses of frogs were known to be used for producing an antibiotic that can heal skin cancer. However, those frog specieses are very hard to find and catch. Therefore, came the need to build a system that can helped with detecting and locating those frogs.

Objectives

1- To implement human voice biometric and frog bioacoustics identification systems with different types of machine learning classifiers.
2- To evaluate and compare the performance of both developed systems for different types of classifiers.

contributions

This study came out with a system that is able to detect the nine different frog species using four different types of machine learning classifiers and two different feature extraction algorithms.

My MS.c. was done under the supervision of Associate Professor Dr. Dzati Athiar Ramli
